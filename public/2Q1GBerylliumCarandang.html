STUDYING TIPS FOR PISAY STUDENTS

Many incoming PSHS students often encounter stories from their fellow schoolmates from upper batches, who say that time-management is a very important thing in the life of a Pisay student. Yet, being time-productive is easier said than done, and many students at first struggle with accomplishing this. Thus, the goal of this webpage is to help students who still struggle to keep their attention and focus in order to be productive, by providing some PRACTICAL tips that I personally find useful myself.

USE PRACTICE EXERCISES

SAMPLE ARCHERS PHYSICS REVIEWER

Practice exercises are a great way to employ "retrivial practice", in which you try to remember information long after since you first read it. I personally use this method a lot especially in topics such as Math and Social Science, as a quick and easy way to study before a test. I find it best that you solve the problems purely mentally, as this will build a strong memory of the topics that you have read, and will also help you actually critically think and analyze the things that you memorize.

Best Practice exercises:

  • ARCHERS' Reviewers
  • Online Reviewers
  • Reviewers made by ChatGPT (useful: you can ask for explanation, solution, etc.)

TAKE ADVANTAGE OF TO-DO LISTS

To-do lists personally help me in organizing and prioritizing tasks, helping me manage my time very efficiently. Why so? I personally like when I have reference that I can rely on, on what tasks to do first, keeping track of how much things I need to do, and helps manage my expectations for certain days. To-do can be useful in many ways. I make to-do lists of all my requirements but I also plan agendas for certain days or for certain weeks through the use of to-do lists. I always update my to-do list after every school day, to enter requirements and tasks given to us on that certain day. It is very nice to keep track of tasks on your own, as you can always trust that the information you are relying on is accurate, instead of relying on the to-do lists of others which you may not know might be inaccurate. This gives ease of mind knowing that you didn't miss any requirements. I personally write to-do list entries in the following format: "[task] due [month][date]]"

Sample:

  • math worksheet due 26 august
  • ss research due 27 august
  • Rchemistry pre lab report due 29 august
  • filipino balagtasan due 28 august
  • valed lesson 2 due 28 august

USE POMODORO TIMER

Pomodoro timers are personally something that I do not use. The reason why I included it in this tutorial was because based on what I heard, it is a very effecient way to manage low attention span caused by neurological disorders such as ADHD, by taking advantage of attention bursts, and giving frequent breaks to regain focus on a certain topic. Although, based on my experience using it before in elementary to study for exams, it was a very efficient way to keep focus and productivity to the max along with using audio and visual background to help you focus even further.
